Features of the NanoManipulator

3D Graphics

Users can easily move, rotate and scale a real 3D view of the height field measured by the Scanning Probe Microscope (SPM). Changeable lighting highlights small surface features. The Z dimension can be scaled independently to exaggerate height changes. Interactivity is maintained at 200x200 resolution or more.

Visualization

Several data layers can be displayed simultaneously using various visualization techniques.

Built-in or user-defined color maps display high-detail data.

Contour-lines allows an additional layer of information. All display methods are updated in real-time as new data arrives from the SPM.

Modification

The NanoManipulator allows precise control of the SPM tip for setting up and executing modifications to the sample. The Phantom™ force-feedback device allows you to feel the shape of the sample as you control the motion of the tip over it, so you know exactly what features the tip is encountering. This allows you to precisely mark locations for the computer to make automatic modifications, or to control the tip in real-time and feel the sample changing as you modify it. The NanoManipulator makes automatic transitions between contact and oscillating modes, so you can image in your sample with the feather touch of non-contact mode, then modify it with a higher setpoint and more force in contact mode. Plus, several types of data are collected during a modification, so you can examine the changes in topography and lateral force as the modification was performed.

Analysis

Several features assist in quantitative analysis of data received from the SPM. First, all data can be exported in several formats, so it can be analyzed or graphed with packages you are already familiar with.

A grid can be superimposed on the surface for quick size comparisons. Three measure lines allow comparison of xy and z differences and angular measurements. A stripchart shows the data collected during a modification in real-time, so you can track the progress of a modification visually, and examine it in more detail after completion.

Layers of data can be manipulated with simple addition or subtraction operations, or "flattened" to remove the slope of a substrate.

Store/Replay

All data taken during an experiment is collected in an automatic lab notebook, so that the entire experiment can be replayed later. Replay of unusual behavior has proved extremely valuable in a research setting, because a new theory will allow re-interpretation when the experiment is replayed. Data can also be extracted at any point during replay, for export to external programs, or for presentation.

Input Devices

The NanoManipulator provides two input devices for working with the 3D virtual environment, the Phantom, and the Magellan. They are designed to be used together, the Phantom stylus in the dominant hand, and the Magellan in the non-dominant hand.
