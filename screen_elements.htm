Perspective

The view of the surface is a perspective view, appropriate for simulating the look of the real world. Objects closer to us are larger than objects far away. This also means that lines which are parallel in 3 dimensions (3D) are sometimes not drawn parallel on your 2D screen. For instance, the Measure Lines and green Aim Line are all parallel, but are not drawn parallel in the picture below.

Their bottom, near the bottom of the screen, are closer together than their tops. This is because their bottoms are farther away from us. If you think of them as parallel, it can help in perceiving the shape and position of things in the virtual environment. In particular, by using the green Aim Line, you can determine where the hand is in relation to the surface, as explained in the Phantom Modes section.

Screen Elements

Various elements of the screen are labeled by numbers in this picture, and discussed below.

  1. Surface - Displays in 3D the height information from the plane selected by the Height Plane control. 
  2. Orientation arrow - This red arrow shows which edge of the surface is "up" in the ThermoMicroscopes software. This edge is where the scan starts, and always appears as the top of the image if the plane data is saved.
  3. Measure Lines - These red, yellow and blue lines start in the corners of the surface, and provide a way to make quick measurements of surface features. See Measure Lines.
  4. The upper left corner of the screen is the time indicator. If you are connected to a live SPM, it says Live(23) where the number indicates the number of seconds you have been connected to the SPM. If you have opened a stream file, it says either Play (23), Play 5x(23), or Pause(23). The multiplier indicates how much faster than real-time you are replaying the stream file, and the number indicates the number of seconds that have elapsed in the recorded experiment. Pause indicates that time is not advancing. If you have opened a ThermoMicroscopes data file or other static data file, it says Static File.
  5. In the top center of the screen is an indicator of the Phantom mode. It provides a quick way to tell what will happen if you press the Phantom button. 
  6. The measure line display show the distances and angles between the three measure lines. These are explained in the section on Measure Lines.
  7. In the lower left corner, the Height Plane name and scale are displayed. On the next line the grid resolution and the size of the current scan area are displayed. And on the line at the bottom, if you are scanning or looking at a stream file, the point on the surface under the hand is displayed. If you are touching the surface, it will display the most recent point data sent by the SPM.
  8. In the lower right corner, the setpoints used for image and modify modes are displayed.
  9. The hand icon represents the position of the Phantom in the virtual environment. Its shapes are described in Phantom Modes.
  10. (Not shown) The scan-line indicator. A green line lies across the surface, and indicates which line the SPM has just scanned, both during live and recorded experiments.
