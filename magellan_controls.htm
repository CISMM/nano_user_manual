When using the Phantom in your right (or dominant) hand, it is essential to be able to change what the Phantom does without putting down the Phantom pen. The Magellan Space Mouse has been provided to fill that function. First, it's nine buttons allow a user of the NanoManipulator to switch the function of the Phantom easily, without taking their attention from the screen. Second, it's puck provides an additional 3D input device which allows positioning of the height plane in the surface-view window.

Buttons

The buttons on the Magellan perform these functions. 

Follow the links below to find out more about these functions.

  2. Position Light, Measure, Scale Up, Scale Down

  4. Commit
  5. Center

  7. XY Lock
  8. Cancel

The * button has the special function of activating the Puck - see below.

Buttons 2 and 3 both activate more that one Phantom mode - they do so by cycling through the modes listed. If you are using Grab mode, and you hit Magellan button 2, you change to Position Light mode. Hit button 2 again and you change to Measure mode. Again and you change to Scale Up, then Scale Down, then Position Light. Button 3 works similarly, cycling between Touch and Select.

Puck

Pressing the * button activates the Puck. If you push or twist the puck, the surface will move in a corresponding direction. This can be used in combination with the Phantom, so it is possible to position the surface with the puck, and move the light direction with the Phantom at the same time. The puck will move the surface when the Phantom is in Grab, Position Light, Scale Up, Scale Down or Measure mode.

On source of confusion is that when the puck is pushed forward, parallel to the surface of the table, you might expect the surface to move up, which is what a mouse cursor would do. Instead there is a direct correspondence between motion of the puck and the surface - the surface moves into the screen. To make the surface move up, lift the puck away from the table. Once you understand this correspondence between puck motion and surface motion, moving the surface becomes much easier.

Special Magellan Modes

The Magellan has some special modes which control the way the Puck moves the surface. They are activated and deactivated by pressing and holding the * key and then pressing another key.

* plus 1 - Disable translation. Only rotations take effect. Press again to re-enable translations. 

* plus 2 - Disable rotations. Only translations take effect. Press again to re-enable rotations. 

* plus 3 - Dominant axis mode. Only the axis with the largest magnitude takes effect. This means if you mostly pull up, but also rotate slightly, only the upward motion takes effect. Press again to allow all axis motion to take effect.

* plus 4 - resets the Magellan. This is an internal reset only, so this will not allow the NanoManipulator to re-connect to the Magellan if it fails to connect when it starts up.

Unfortunately, the * key still activates and deactivates the Puck when used for these commands. So if the puck is active and you hit * plus 1 to disable translation, you will have to hit the * key again to re-enable the Puck.
