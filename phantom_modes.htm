The PHANTOM™ is a haptic interface, produced by SensAble Technologies, that allows you to interact in 3D with the NanoManipulator. 

In general, hold the Phantom like you would a ballpoint pen, with your index finger on the button. This will give you precise control over the Phantom's position and easy access to its button. With it, for example, you can:

move the image around in 3D (see Grabbing)

scale the image up and down (see Scaling Up and Down)

feel items in the image (see Touch)

You can use the Phantom button much as you would a mouse’s button. 

When the Phantom is first turned on, it is in Grab mode.  Its pointer (commonly referred to as the hand) looks like a small, multicolored cube.  If at first you don’t see this cube, try moving the Phantom stylus around.  The cube may be off screen, or hidden by the surface.

Watch the cube move, as you try moving the Phantom’s stylus up and down, right and left, backwards and forwards.

The Magellan is designed to work in concert with the Phantom. The buttons on the Magellan can be pressed using your non-dominant hand, changing the task you are performing without putting down the Phantom stylus. Each Phantom mode described below is mapped to a button on the Magellan, indicated by the label of the box enclosing the button, as described in the Magellan Controls page. 

The Green Aim Line

Several modes include a green line that follows the motion of the hand icon. This line is provided as an aid to determining the 3D position of the hand. It is always perpendicular to the surface and parallel to the Measure Lines, and it always intersects the surface. If the hand is above or below the surface, it also intersects the hand. If the hand is off to the side of the surface, it moves as close as it can to the hand while still intersecting the surface. 

Here are some examples which show how it can help determine the position of the hand.

In this picture, the hand is off to the side of the surface, so the green aim line is up against the edge of the surface, and doesn't appear to intersect the hand icon.

Here the hand is above the surface, so you can see where the aim line intersects both the surface and the hand icon. The length of line in between helps show how far above the surface the hand is. 

Here the hand is intersecting the surface. Notice that no green line is visible between the hand icon and the surface.

Grab/Scale

You can use the Phantom to grab the surface and move it around, so that features are more easily visible. For this to be possible, the NanoManipulator must be in grab mode.  The NanoManipulator starts in grab mode.  If you have been working in another mode and would like to return to grab mode, just click Grab in the main control window.  The diamond will turn red, indicating that you are working in grab mode.

The hand, or pointer, looks like a small, multicolored box.  If at first you don’t see this box, try  moving the Phantom stylus around.  The box may be off screen, or hidden by the image plane.

If grab mode is activated, then whenever the Phantom button is pressed and held down and the Phantom is moved, the surface will follow the motion of the Phantom.

Center

Centering the surface brings the surface to the middle of the graphics window, and standardizes its size, viewpoint and lighting.  The surface is placed at a 45 degree angle to the user, and the light is comes from directly overhead. It undoes the effects of any grab, scale up, scale down or position light operation that happened before.  This is often useful when the surface is at a strange size or viewpoint, or when it is not visible.  

To center the surface, simply click on the button marked Center, which is located in the bottom left-hand corner of the main control window.

Position Light

Changing the lighting can affect the way in which your image is perceived. To begin positioning the light, click Position Light on the main control window. The light icon is a lamp shade like that from a desk lamp, with some yellow lines coming out indicating the light direction.

Press the Phantom stylus button and twist and rotate the Phantom stylus to move the direction of the light.  When you are satisfied with the lighting, release the button, and the light will stay where it is.

The only motion of the Phantom which changes the light is twist and rotation: its position doesn't matter. The light is directional, similar to the sun, with all its light rays parallel.

Measure

This tool allows you to use the red, yellow and blue lines on the surface to make quick measurements of surface features. It is explained in the section Measure Lines.

Scale

The Phantom can be used to scale images up and down.  This can be useful when you would like to see “the bigger picture,” or when you want to focus in on a particular detail.  Click on Scale in the main control window.  

The pointer in Scale mode looks like a pair of two green arrows, with their tails back-to-back.  If at first you don’t see a pair of arrows, try moving the Phantom stylus around.  The arrows may be off screen, or hidden by the image plane. 

Put the intersection of the green aim line with the surface at the point on which you wish to zoom in or out.  Press the stylus button, and then move the pen towards yourself to scale up (pull the object towards you). To scale down, push the pen towards the Phantom body (push the object away from you). An extreme change in scale may require you to pull or push twice. 

Touch Stored

In touch stored mode, you can use the Phantom to feel items within the on-screen image.  To enable touch stored mode, click on the diamond beside the words Touch Stored in the main control window.  (It is located in the lower right-hand corner.)  The diamond will turn red, indicating that you are working in touch stored mode.

Touch stored mode’s pointer looks like a blue cone.  If at first you don’t see this cone, try moving the Phantom stylus around.  The cone may be off screen, or hidden by the image plane.

Now press the Phantom button.  Keep the button pressed down as you slowly touch the tip of the cone to items you wish to feel.  Continue to hold the button down as you move the tip along the surface.  You will be able to feel resistance from the Phantom whenever you touch the surface.

It is important to note that in this mode you are not controlling the SPM's tip. Instead, you are feeling what is visible on the screen - the data from the most recent scan, displayed as the surface. Touch mode allows you to control the SPM tip directly.

The red ball at the blue cone's tip is automatically sized to represent a 12.5 nm sphere. It is there to provide some idea of the size of a sharp AFM tip. The Display Settings dialog allows you to change the size of this sphere, if desired. 

Region

In region mode, you can select a part of the surface to focus on and see in complete detail, while blurring areas outside that area to make it easier for the computer to draw. This allows you to collect high resolution data (500x500 or 1000x1000), and still maintain interactive speeds while using the Phantom. 

Region mode's pointer looks like a blue arrow. Put the intersection of the green aim line with the surface at the point on which you wish to focus. Press and hold the stylus button, and drag a rectangular area around the central point. A box indicates where the current region is located, and areas outside this region become blurry. Release the stylus button. 

Now that the region is placed, its size can be adjusted by dragging the edges of the region. When the green aim line is near enough to an edge to drag it, that edge will "highlight" by becoming more transparent. There is a box in the center of the region, too - if the green aim line is inside it, it become more transparent, and you can press the Phantom button to translate and rotate the region. If you click away from an edge and away from the central box, a new region will be created. 

Note that if you switch to other Phantom modes, like touch or grab, the region will remain active, allowing you to do modifications on a detailed region of a high resolution scan at interactive rates. You can switch back to the region tool to adjust the position of the region at any time. 

To delete the region so the entire surface is displayed in full detail, either translate the region entirely off the surface, or click with the aim line entirely off the surface. 

Touch

This mode is only available when the NanoManipulator is connected to a "live" SPM - you must have connected to your ThermoMicroscopes SPM.

Touch allows you to touch the sample in your SPM using the Phantom. This mode is where the haptic (touch) capabilities of the Phantom come in most useful. When you press the button, the SPM tip will stop scanning the sample, and will move to the XY position of your hand. The SPM tip stays in feedback, and stays on the surface of the sample, using the Imaging parameters you were using to scan the sample.

Because the SPM tip remains in feedback, it does not follow the Phantom's motions in the Z axis, or perpendicular to the scan plane.

You can easily tell the XY position of your hand by using the green line which follows you hand. It intersects both your hand icon (the blue cone) and the surface. The amount of green line between the blue cone and the surface shows you how far above the surface your hand is. Normally the icon is the same as the touch stored icon, but when you use the sweep tool, these two additional green lines appear to represent the width of the sweep you can perform. 

To touch the surface, press the button, and move your hand down until the blue cone rests on the surface. It may stop slightly above or below the surface, if the SPM is experiencing drift.

What happens next depends on what kind of modification you have set up in the Modify controls window. Also see the NanoManipulator Tutorial for several examples of how to perform a modification. 

The red ball at the blue cone's tip is automatically sized to represent a 12.5 nm sphere. It is there to provide some idea of the size of a sharp AFM tip. The Display Settings dialog allows you to change the size of this sphere, if desired. 

Select

This mode is only available when the NanoManipulator is connected to a "live" SPM - you must have connected to your ThermoMicroscopes SPM.

Select allows you to change the region the SPM is scanning. It is similar to the Zoom/Translate tool provided by the ThermoMicroscopes SPMlab software.

First decide roughly where you would like the new scan region to be. Then position your hand so its XY position is in the center of that region. Then press the Phantom button, and drag to one corner of the new region. You will see a box grow that marks the boundaries of the new scan region. Release the button when you are satisfied.

Now, if you press the commit button, the SPM will start scanning the new region you just marked out.

If you press the cancel button, the scan region will remain the same, and you can drag a different region. You can also simply start over by dragging a new region.

XY Lock

This is a toggle button which prevents the Phantom from moving the hand icon or the SPM tip during some modifications. It allows you to precisely position the SPM tip and then lock its position. This means you can acquire repeated measurements from the same point, or change the force applied by the tip with it locked in place.

Commit

This button is used by the Select mode and by Touch mode to approve an operation. In select mode, it approves the new scan region, and in touch mode, it starts a modification. See the NanoManipulator Tutorial and Modify controls for examples of how to use the commit button.

Cancel

This button is used by the Select mode and by Touch mode to back out of an operation. In select mode, it rejects the new scan region, and in touch mode, it backs out of a modification and begins scanning the sample again. See the NanoManipulator Tutorial and Modify controls for examples of how to use the cancel button.
