The Display Settings dialog controls the basic properties of the surface and icons used in the 3D view of your sample. Most of the time it is not necessary to adjust these parameters, but changing them can provide some insight into how the 3D view of your sample is generated. 

Specular lighting is view-dependent. It gives white highlights which are reflected off the surface from the light source, and change if you grab the surface or move the light. Diffuse lighting is not view-dependent, and contains the base color of the surface, from the color map or surface color. You can adjust the relative amounts of these lights with the two controls for Amount of specular lighting and Amount of diffuse lighting. In general, the number entered in these two boxes should add up to 1.0 or slightly above, but they don't have to. 

You can also control how sharp a highlight specular lighting produces with the highlight sharpness control. The default value of 55 is considered normal for a moderately polished surface.

The Shading box controls how an individual triangle is shaded. Smooth shading interpolates the colors at the three corners of the triangle across it's surface, while flat shading forces a single color for the whole triangle. 

The Light Source box controls the type of light used. A "small light" will create a highlight on the surface, with areas outside the highlight not lit as brightly. A "large light" will illuminate the whole surface in the same way, avoiding a highlight only on one part of the surface. 

The Surface box lets you display the triangles that make up the surface either as solid or wire-frame, as an illustration of how many data points are being displayed on the surface. The corners of each triangle lie at a data point. If the Detail/Speed control is set to the most detail, the surface is displaying all the scan data that is collected. You can also make the surface partially transparent by setting the Surface Opacity control to a number less than 1.0. You might want to do this if you need to see the hand icon below the displayed surface.

Finally, you can change the size of the hand icons with the Icon scale control. The red sphere at the tip of the Touch icon has a different control, because it is automatically sized to have a 12.5 nm diameter when the surface is displaying data in nanometers. You can change its size with the Sphere scale control.
