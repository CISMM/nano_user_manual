The measure lines can be used to measure height and distance.  There are three measure lines: red, yellow, and blue.  They start out in three different corners.  The red one begins at the lower left-hand corner of the surface, the yellow one starts at the lower right-hand corner, and the blue on starts in the upper right-hand corner.

Imagine the measure lines as being perfectly perpendicular to some idealized, flattened surface – and therefore perfectly parallel to each other.

In the upper right-hand corner of the screen, you should see four short colored lines, with white numbers and letters between them.  The numbers labeled Dxy are the lateral distance between the measure lines.  In the image above, the red and yellow lines are a lateral distance of 6248 nm from each other, as are the yellow and blue lines.  The blue and red lines are 8836 nm apart.  (The measure lines form the vertices of a triangle, the hypotenuse of which is 8836 nm.)

Below this, you should see three numbers labeled Dz, one for each measure line.  The Dz numbers are the vertical distance between the points where the measure lines intersect the surface.  (If the entire surface was flat, all of the Dz numbers would be zero.)

When you are ready to move the measure lines, click “Measure” on the main control window.

Your Phantom pointer will turn into a box whose color matches the nearest measure line.

Try moving the Phantom around.  Your box will change colors to match whichever measure line is closest. Remember that you are moving the Phantom in 3 dimensions, so movements into and out of the screen also affect which measure line is closest. 

To pick up the nearest measure line, simply push the button on the Phantom stylus.  The line will “snap to” your box.  When you let go of the button, you drop the measure line.  When you press it in, you pick it up again.  If you move the Phantom around while the measure line is “picked up,” the measure line will follow your box.

Before you begin measuring things at a scaled up range, it is a good idea to move all of your measure lines to one location, so that they will be easy to find and retrieve.

Measure lines are used in combination with two other tools. They are used to specify three points that form a flatten plane, as explained in Calculate Data Planes. They also can be used to position and orient the Rulergrid.
