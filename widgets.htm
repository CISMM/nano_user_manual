The NanoManipulator uses a widget, the entry box, to obtain input values from the user.

Using Entry Boxes

Entry boxes are a type of widget that allows you to type a value directly into a window. An example of an entry box, the Z scale entry box, is shown below.


To change the Z scale value, first click on the Z scale box and backspace to delete the old number. The box will turn pink.

Type the new z-scale value, and press Enter, or click in another entry box.  The box will turn white as the new number takes effect.

Using pop-up selectors

Pop up selectors let you choose a value from a list. They look like a button, except they have a bar on the right edge. If you click the left mouse button, all the choices will be displayed:

Drag or click again to choose one of the items in the list. 

You can also click the right mouse button over the widget, and it will cycle through the available selections.
